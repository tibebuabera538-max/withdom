basic work of Ethio Telecom

Ethio Telecom is the oldest and largest telecommunications provider in Ethiopia. In 2026, its "working" or operational infrastructure is defined by a massive shift from traditional voice services to a high-speed digital ecosystem. Here is a breakdown of how Ethio Telecom functions across its core areas Network Infrastructure & CoverageAs of early 2026, Ethio Telecom has achieved nearly universal population coverage, utilizing a mix of satellite, fiber, and mobile technologies. Population Coverage: Approximately 99.8% of the Ethiopian population is covered by their network. Mobile Generations:5G: Currently operational in over 25 major cities, providing ultra-high-speed internet for businesses and advanced "Smart City" projects. 4G LTE: This is the backbone of their data service, reaching over 80% of the population and covering more than 1,060 towns.3G/2G: Still maintained for older devices and basic voice services in remote rural areas.Fiber Backbone They manage over 23,000 km of inland fiber optic cables. To ensure international connectivity, these lines run through Sudan, Djibouti, Kenya, and Somaliland to connect to global undersea cables. International ConnectivityTo keep Ethiopia connected to the global internet, the company uses a strategy called Local Caching alongside physical gateways.International Gateways (IGW) Their total capacity has reached roughly Tbps (Terabits per second). Local Caching: About half of the data traffic (like YouTube, TikTok, and Meta) is stored on servers inside Ethiopia. This means when you watch a video, the data comes from a local server rather than a cable across the ocean, which makes your internet faster and saves the country foreign currency.Satellite The Sululta Earth Station remains a critical backup and primary link for international satellite communication. The "Telebirr" EcosystemEthio Telecom is no longer just a "phone company"; it functions as a massive financial hub through telebirr. Mobile Money: It allows millions of users to pay for utilities, shop, and transfer money without a traditional bank account. Digital Integration It is now integrated into national infrastructure, such as paying for expressway tolls (RFID-powered) and government service fees.Modernization Strategy (Next Horizon 2028)The company is currently executing its "Next Horizon: Digital & Beyond" strategy. Key technical focuses include Copper Switch-Off Systematically replacing old copper telephone wires with high-speed Fiber-to-the-Home (FTTH). Energy Efficiency To combat power outages, they have deployed over 30MW of solar power to keep mobile towers running in rural areas.Cloud & AI: Using AI-assisted systems to manage network traffic and offering cloud hosting services for local businesses via their Huawei-built data centers.
